U-Net GAN Framework

Our core engine is a deep U-Net Generator paired with a PatchGAN Discriminator. This architecture excels at translation tasks, converting coarse global atmospheric signals into fine-grained local precipitation maps. The adversarial training ensures sharp, realistic gradients that traditional regression models often "blur" into averages.

Fused Datasets

We perform Domain Fusion: leveraging ERA5-T for dynamic drivers (200hPa Geopotential, Specific Humidity, Wind Vectors, and SST) and CHIRPS for historical ground-truth. By training on 20+ years of high-resolution IR-based rainfall, the model learns the unique topographic influences on Ethiopia's micro-climates.

CI/CD Operational Logic

The system is "Alive." Using GitHub Actions and the CDS API, ET-NeuralCast fetches the latest preliminary reanalysis data every Monday. It automatically normalizes inputs against a 20-year per-pixel climatology, generates forecasts, and updates this dashboard with no manual intervention required.

Categorical Probability

Tercile Classification

Divides the forecast into three categories: Below Normal, Near Normal, and Above Normal. This uses spatial-weekly probability thresholds to classify the coming week relative to the 20-year distribution.

Precipitation Anomaly

Anomaly Magnitude

Shows the departure from the historical mean in mm/day. This view is critical for early warning systems, highlighting regions where rainfall is expected to be significantly higher or lower than the seasonal norm.

Total Physical Output

Absolute Precipitation

The final processed prediction of absolute rainfall depth in mm/day. This value is derived by inverse-transforming the model's standardized outputs using local spatial-weekly standard deviations.

Percent of Normal

Relative Moisture

A normalized view showing the forecast as a percentage of the usual rainfall for this specific week. Values above 100% indicate wetter-than-usual conditions, regardless of the absolute rainfall depth.
