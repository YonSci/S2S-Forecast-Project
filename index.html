ET-NeuralCast

Sub-seasonal to Seasonal Forecasting for Ethiopia using Deep Learning

Operational

Hybrid Deep Learning Framework

The ET-NeuralCast architecture is a Hybrid Deep Learning framework specifically designed for Sub-seasonal to Seasonal (S2S) downscaling. Fusing the Generator (U-Net) and the Discriminator (PatchGAN) to capture the spatiotemporal patterns of the input climate data.

Intelligent Data Fusion

Built on a 4D tensor of atmospheric and oceanic predictors: Geopotential (Z500), Humidity (Q), Wind Vectors (U/V), and Sea Surface Temperature (SST), all cross-referenced with 0.05° CHIRPS precipitation history.

Daily Production Pipeline

Fully autonomous CI/CD pipelines running every 24 hours at 00:00 UTC. The system automatically orchestrates data acquisition, inference, and multi-perspective forecast generation.

Technical Framework

Data Pipeline

Preprocessing & Normalization

Sources: ERA5 reanalysis and CHIRPS precipitation datasets. Performs clipping to Ethiopia's bounding box and weekly mean aggregation.

  • Anomaly Creation: Subtracts training-set-only weekly climatology.
  • Standardization: Zero-mean/unit-variance scaling using training statistics.
  • Lagged Predictors: Captures temporal atmospheric memory.

Input Format

Tensors: [Batch, Time, Variable, Lat, Lon]. Includes Z500, Q850, U/V Wind, and SST.

Model Architecture

U-Net Generator Core

Deep encoder-decoder with skip connections ensuring preservation of both local and contextual atmospheric features.

  • Encoder: Progressive downsampling to capture planetary patterns.
  • Decoder: High-res reconstruction using transposed convolutions.
  • Tanh Activation: Outputs normalized precipitation anomalies.

PatchGAN Discriminator

Adversarial "critic" evaluating 16x16 spatial patches for absolute physical realism.

Training Strategy

Phase 1: Warm Start

Generator-only training using L1 Loss to stabilize core spatial patterns and ensure reliable baseline predictions.

Phase 2: GAN Fine-tuning

Full adversarial cycle. Generator is tuned to both minimize pixel-wise error and fool the Discriminator.

  • Loss Profile: Weighted combination of L1 and MSE PatchGAN loss.
  • Leakage Prevention: Chronological training splits to ensure zero data leakage.

Autonomous Operation

End-to-End CI/CD

Fully automated Docker-ready pipeline hosted via GitHub Actions, running every 24 hours at 00:00 UTC.

  • Ingestion: Real-time fetching of ERA5-T atmospheric drivers.
  • Sync: On-the-fly normalization matching training statistics.
  • Deployment: Immediate update of dashboard PNGs and NetCDF exports.

Total latency: ~45 mins from raw atmospheric data to published forecast.

Operational Forecasts

Anomaly Forecast

Precipitation Anomaly

Identifying regions of critical rainfall deficit or surplus by calculating deviation from the mean in physical units (mm/day).

Total Precipitation Forecast

Absolute Physical Flux

The total expected water volume reaching the surface. Reconstructed from the learned anomaly and regional seasonal baseline data.

Percent of Normal

Percent of Operational Normal

A relative moisture metric expressing the forecast as a percentage of standard expectation, highlighting extreme departures from the norm.

Tercile Probability Forecast

Tercile Probability

Classification of seasonal expectations into Below, Near, or Above normal states based on local 20-year climatology thresholds.
